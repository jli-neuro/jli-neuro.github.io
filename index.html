About Me

I am currently a PhD researcher at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai. My work began in clinical neuroscience, studying patient behaviors, but I realized that understanding "dysfunction" requires rigorous models of how intelligent systems form beliefs about their own capabilities.

Today, I use Reinforcement Learning and Bayesian Inference to model how agents (human or artificial) maintain internal models of competence vs. uncertainty. My goal is to develop frameworks for AI Capability Calibration—preventing confidently stated hallucinations in high-stakes settings.

Here are a few technologies I've been working with:

  • Python (PyTorch)
  • Hierarchical RL
  • Bayesian Modeling
  • R / Stan
  • Behavioral Task Design
  • Generative Models

Self-Efficacy as Inference
Modeling self-efficacy not as a static trait, but as a dynamic belief state over latent competence. Simulations show how this regulates exploration-exploitation in sequential decision making.

The "Mania" Attractor
Demonstrating how maladaptive belief updates create attractor-like dynamics resembling bipolar mania. Using computational failures to understand clinical instability.

AI Capability Calibration
