About

I’m a neuroscience PhD candidate studying how people learn what they can and cannot do—and how those beliefs shape motivation, planning, and decision‑making.

My work connects Bandura’s self‑efficacy theory with reinforcement learning and Bayesian learning. I’m particularly interested in how inflated value propagation and miscalibration can relate to reward generalization and mania‑risk traits.

Research

I build models and experiments around a simple question: how does an agent learn a calibrated belief about its own capability? That belief can regulate goal selection, effort, reward generalization, and planning under uncertainty.

Self‑efficacy as a learning signal

Formalizing self‑efficacy updates and how they interact with extrinsic value learning.

Reward generalization tasks

Behavioral assays linking generalization parameters to self‑reported traits (e.g., POG).

Planning under internal uncertainty

How motor noise and capability uncertainty should shape planning and action selection.
